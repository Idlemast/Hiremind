# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo recruiters or small recruitment teams working under time pressure. They review high volumes of candidates in concentrated sessions — assigning scores, deciding who advances, managing multiple open requisitions simultaneously. Decision fatigue is the core enemy: too many profiles, too little signal, too much cognitive overhead per review.

## Product Purpose

HireMind is a candidate triage and decision support tool (a "System of Clarity"). It scores candidates automatically against job requirements, surfaces highest-signal profiles first, and structures the recruiter's review process to reduce cognitive load. Success looks like: fewer qualified candidates missed, less time wasted on obvious mismatches, and each hiring decision backed by visible reasoning rather than gut feel.

Runs locally (SQLite, no auth) for the MVP. No external ATS dependency.

## Brand Personality

Calm · Precise · Trustworthy

Voice: authoritative but not cold, supportive without being patronizing. Tone: clear, direct, minimal. The tool is a silent partner — it provides structure and signal so the recruiter's judgment stays at the center.

## Anti-references

- **Heavy enterprise SaaS** (Workday, SAP, Greenhouse) — dark sidebars, dense navigation trees, corporate gray, feature sprawl. Avoid the "IT-deployed toolkit" aesthetic.
- **Consumer/social SaaS polish** (Notion, Linear, Loom) — bubbly minimalism, rounded-everything, playful micro-motion, personality-forward empty states. Avoid hipster-SaaS energy; this is a professional decision tool.

The target sits between those poles: structured and credible without feeling bureaucratic, clean and modern without feeling like a product demo.

## Design Principles

1. **Cognitive relief first** — never add visual complexity without a clear function. Whitespace is load-bearing.
2. **Data before chrome** — content drives hierarchy; UI scaffolding recedes. The recruiter reads candidates, not the interface.
3. **Systematic but human** — the tool's structure supports the recruiter's judgment, it doesn't replace it. Candidate data should feel like people, not rows.
4. **Signal, not decoration** — semantic color (emerald/amber/red for fit signals) is used purposefully and sparingly. Color as noise is worse than no color.
5. **Timeless over trendy** — design choices that hold across product cycles. No trend-chasing; clarity ages better than novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast against backgrounds. Reduced motion respected. No color-only information encoding (fit signals always carry a label alongside color).
